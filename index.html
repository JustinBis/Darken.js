Darken.js

Nightmode Made Easy

Working Working at night is tough enough already, so why make your eyes suffer more than they have to?
Most websites show dark text on a bright background. This is great during the day, but at night, our eyes need a break.
That's where Darken.js comes in.

Darken.js can be added to any website with nothing more than a few javascript includes. 

                        <script src="//code.jquery.com/jquery-1.11.0.min.js"></script>
                        <script src="/tinycolor.js"></script>
                        <script src="/darken.js"></script>

jQuery and tinycolor.js are requirements for Darken.js to work.

Once included, Darken.js will add a small header at the top of the page asking users if they want to enter night mode.
If the page's text is determined to be dark and the background bright, we darken the background and brighten the text. This makes the text far easier to read at night and causes less eye fatigue. Of course, this doesn't affect images, as inverted color images just look silly, even at night.
